Permanent-Life Insurance

Published On 25th March 2023

    Permanent life insurance is a type of life insurance policy that provides coverage for the entire lifetime of the policyholder, as long as the premiums are paid. Unlike term life insurance policies, permanent life insurance policies do not have a set expiration date.

    One of the primary advantages of permanent life insurance is that it builds cash value over time. As premiums are paid, a portion of the premium goes into a savings component of the policy, which earns interest or investment returns. This cash value can be borrowed against or used to pay premiums, and can even be surrendered for cash. This means that permanent life insurance policies can serve as a form of savings or investment for the policyholder.

    Another advantage of permanent life insurance is that it provides a guaranteed death benefit. As long as the premiums are paid, the policy will pay a death benefit to the beneficiaries named in the policy. This can provide peace of mind for policyholders who want to ensure their loved ones are financially protected in the event of an unexpected death.

There are several types of permanent life insurance policies, including:

  1. Whole Life Insurance: Whole life insurance provides a guaranteed death benefit and builds cash value over time. The premiums for this type of insurance are fixed for the life of the policy and the cash value grows tax-deferred.
  2. Universal Life Insurance: Universal life insurance is a type of permanent life insurance that allows policyholders to adjust the death benefit and premiums over time. This type of insurance is often more flexible than whole life insurance and allows policyholders to build cash value through an interest-bearing account.
  3. Variable Life Insurance: Variable life insurance is a type of permanent life insurance that allows policyholders to invest the cash value of their policy in a variety of investment options such as stocks, bonds, and mutual funds. The death benefit and cash value of this type of policy will vary depending on the performance of the underlying investments.

    In conclusion, permanent life insurance is a type of policy that provides coverage for the entire lifetime of the policyholder and builds cash value over time. It offers a guaranteed death benefit and can serve as a form of savings or investment for the policyholder. However, permanent life insurance policies are generally more expensive than term life insurance policies and may not be the best option for individuals who want to keep their premiums as low as possible. It's important to speak with a licensed insurance agent to determine the best type of coverage for your individual needs and goals.
